This word is used to describe how an arc or a chord creates an angle at a point on the circle. For example, when an arc forms an angle at the circumfrance, we say the angle "subtended" at the circumfrance by the arc.

#### Theorem 3 - Angles in a Semicircle is always 90 degrees

If an arc is a semicircle, that is, the arc lies on the circles diameter. Then, the angle subtended on the circumfrance will always be 90 degrees.

<div align='center'>
    <img src='./images/23.png'>
</div>

We can create two isoceles triangles to form an algebraic equation.

<div align='center'>
    <img src='./images/24.png'>
</div>

\[
    2a + 2b = 180
\]

Therefore,

\[
    \boxed{a + b = 90}
\]

We also know this to be true from the second theorem, because this point is from the centre equaling 180 degrees, the angle from the circumance must be half this and therefore 90.


#### Theorem 4 - Opposite Angles on A Cyclic Quadrilateral sum to 180

A quadrilateral is called cyclic is all four of its verticies lie on the circumfrance of a circle. This important property creates the theorem that states that the opposite angles always sum to 180.

#### Theorem 5 - Two Tangent Theorem

A tangent is defined as a straight line that touches the circle at exactly one point without crossing it. The important property of a tangent is that it **is always perpendicular to the radius** at the point where it touches the circle.

The two tangent theorem that if two tangents are drawn to a circle from a single external point, **then those tangents will always be equal in length**.

<div align='center'>
    <img src='./images/27.png'>
</div>

Another important property of this theorem is that the line joining the external point to the center of the circle always bisects the angle formed between the two tangents.
